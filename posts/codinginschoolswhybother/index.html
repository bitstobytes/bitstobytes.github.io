Code in School - Why Bother?

By Amy Diaz
@_amydiaz
August 8 2020 | 3 minute read
Tags: codeclub primary educationissues


I initially had no clue what to write about when starting this post.

So I figured I may as well think of why it is that I even like or got into programming in the first place.

The first thing that I realised that if it hadn’t been for the computer lab that was set up and a passionate temp teacher in primary school starting a makeshift coding club at lunchtimes, I probably never would have had an interest in computers or coding, never would have decided to continue teaching myself once she left the school, never would have paid attention in year 7 technology classes, and most likely would not have chosen IST as a year 9 elective.

Who knows what I’d be doing now?

And all this stemmed from one small, seemingly insignificant coding club.

But the computer lab was only there for maybe a year or two before the school got rid of many of its computers in favour of new tablets that could be used in the classroom, and the coding club only existed for a couple months because the year ended and the teacher left the school. Neither of my younger sisters got to do much, if any, computing in primary school. There were no other teachers that had enough free time to run a club, and at any rate, there probably wouldn’t have been the resources to run one anyway. In fact, when I asked my sister if she had done any sort of digital technology type activities at primary school she couldn’t recall that she had done anything much, other than some scratch taught by a classroom teacher who herself was learning how to use it, and maybe some cool math games in the library on a rainy day.

When I last heard, my old school now has a company come in for a weekly lego/robotics club, but no teacher that liked computing, so there was no opportunity for kids who cant afford the extra classes or who aren’t able to go to an after school activity. Which is a shame, because a lot of these kids won’t be able to have (at least what I would say) is a valuable opportunity for learning.

Unfortunately, as is the case with many public primary schools, unless there is a teacher at the school with a passion for computing, and unless the school has a computer lab or a class set of laptops, it becomes really difficult for any sort of easily accessible computing program to be run.

So I’ll leave you all with this…

How can we introduce coding as a fun subject to younger kids?

    - Amy
