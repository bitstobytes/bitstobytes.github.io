Part One: Here is Why You Should Explore Entrepreneurship

By Dillon de Silva
@dillondesilvaa
September 18 2020 | 2 minute read
Tags: entrepreneurship startups inspiration


Youth is a springing blossom. It’s the perfect season of life to experience several of many firsts. First true friendships. First crush. First setback. First feeling of immense pressure as exams can creep up on you.

Entrepreneurship is commonly associated with money, exchanges of cash in the millions and talks of stocks and shares.

When in reality, being an entrepreneur really only entails two things. The most familiar to many is profit.

The other one is risk.

As a young person, you will hopefully have limited liabilities such as running a family or paying rent.

This is your time to try new things.

By venturing out, developing a business doing something you like and attempting to profit from it, you are in for a rewarding experience full of ups and downs. You will develop a great set of skills, be able to understand people better and grow your resilience.

Personally, I’ve attempted to develop several tech startups since 2016. Ironically, even Bits to Bytes is a project I am working on with a team of 5. Through this, I believe I’ve greatly grown my leadership capabilities and tackled several different problems.

I encourage everyone to give entrepreneurship a go. Whether it’s setting up your own juice store, a Shopify site or a blog, venture out into the open and try new things.

In part two of this article, I will be giving some more in depth details as to the simple things you can do to start your first entrepreneurship venture.

We are all born with the capability to become entrepreneurs. All you have to do is make a start :)

    - Dillon
