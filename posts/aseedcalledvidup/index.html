A Seed Called VidUp

By Jason Yu
@jason.yu01
October 30 2020 | 3 minute read
Tags: entrepreneurship stem inspiration


When I first got into programming I was bewildered by the near infinite things one could accomplish through code. And like many people in the beginning, I struggled with the basic concepts. There was a strong sense of uncertainty within me at the time. I distinctly remember thinking, “Am I really cut out for coding? There’s other people way better than me already.”

This sense of uncertainty is normal for anyone learning a new skill. But it is crucial to realize that this initial fear is just the beginning of a journey. With entrepreneurship you will go on many journeys. So for everyone reading, seek that specific feeling out. Like a muscle, mental stress can facilitate growth.

Here’s a funny little story from around that time that relates to the above idea.

One day, my robotics teacher was teaching us python functions and after the lesson he suggested to a few students including myself to work on a small side project. We would be creating a plywood box with the help of the new laser printer that our school had just bought. Immediately I got to writing 30 “if statements”, creating a needlessly complex piece of code. It was essentially a bowl of spaghetti topped off with a mess of mozzarella. I only realised later after sinking in a couple hours of work why our teacher had taught us functions. To solve new and interesting problems you have to be willing to try new things.

My experiences with a multitude of side projects, just like the one above, led me to take my first step into entrepreneurship near the end of 2019. Collaborating with a group of close friends from highschool we sought to create an automated video editing solution that would change the game. The idea was spurred on by a close friend on the team who had created a channel with over 200k subscribers generating automated reddit content. Realizing that there was a gap in the market we pursued the idea of a more generalised video automation solution that could be used by anyone. We would make video creation as easy as the press of a button. There have been many failures along the way; I like to call them iterations; but if the day ever comes that VidUp gets outdone, I will rest easy knowing that I have acquired new skills and have grown as a person.

Finally for anyone interested in pursuing entrepreneurship, I would recommend doing a few things. First, find other like minded people with a similar passion and learn off of each other. Second, don’t be afraid to fail. Failures are only the beginnings of success. Finally, whatever you do, don’t become a spectator in life. Opportunity goes to those willing to take a bull by the horns.

TLDR: Embrace uncertainty, it’s the seeds of future success.

    - Jason
