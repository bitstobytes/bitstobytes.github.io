Bailey's Story

By Bailey Stoodley
@baileystoodley
August 2 2020 | 2 minute read
Tags: opportunity friendships mining


My love for stem came from meeting one of my best friends, Tom Day, back in 2016. He got me into computer programming.

This opened a whole lot of doors in opportunities for me, such as attending my first academic camp hosted by the National Computer Science School in 2019. Through this, I found a love for all things STEM! This camp was honestly one of the best experiences of my life.

I met some of the most inspiring and funniest people (one of the highlights was seeing actually seeing the Bits to Bytes Founder, Dillon, react to an anonymous love letter prank).

Later on, I applied for a Make It Now in Engineering Camp in Mt Isa, QLD. At a glance, I applied for the engineering part. I was accepted, and got to work on the placement of a return water pipeline around a tailings dam; tailings is a SUPER toxic and smelly sludge as a result of refining ores. This would be where I learnt the most about STEM and the heart of Australiaâ€™s economy. One fact I learnt was that most mines have to completely restore the land once completing the mining.

However, little did I know, this camp would change many things and redirect my life.

I was accepted as a Queensland Minerals and Energy Ambassador, awarded highly commended in Exceptional QMEA Female student, and had been accepted to work experience for Glencore in IT. Whilst I thought STEM was just doctors, engineers, mathematicians, and the like, I never realised that Mining was one of the biggest STEM fields in Australia.

Another honourable experience I was able to attend with Tom was the QUT Stem Camp where I worked on developing code that responded to visual stimulus and the creation and administering of medicine. I honestly think the best thing about this was having my mate with me, and stirring up trouble.

I want this story to let you know there are always opportunities to get involved in the STEM fields. If you are interested in STEM, I would totally recommend applying to anything as I can guarantee it will be a super valuable experience in terms of careers and life long memories.

And wonderful friendships ;)

    - Bailey
