Code Ninjas

By Sofia de Bellis
@sofia.debellis
August 3 2020 | 3 minute read
Tags: codeclub studentstartup inspiration


The launch of the code club “Code Ninjas” founded by students Giuliana De Bellis and Edwina Adisusila in 2018 saw an exciting opportunity for extra-curricular computer programming at St Ursula’s College Kingsgrove.

Code Ninjas was first developed after Giuliana and Edwina attended the National Computer Science school in 2018. This camp inspired the girls to take their newfound passion and skills to the students at our school. Coming back from the camp, they brainstormed possible ways to get the college community involved in coding. They reflected on what they learnt and what they wanted to achieve, and came to the conclusion that starting a lunchtime coding club would provide St Ursula’s with just the right opportunity.

Before pitching the club to teachers, the girls reflected on why they thought it was so important. They remembered how enjoyable it was to work together to solve our coding problems and create projects. They thought if they could create a space where like-minded girls could also find this experience, it would foster their curiosity and interest in coding.

Our school didn’t have any student-run clubs before Code Ninjas. This made the girls pretty nervous to introduce this new activity to the school. However, they were met with encouraging support by both IT and non-IT teachers alike, and had plenty of help setting upthe club and advertising it to the school community.

They began the process of creating posters, speaking at year assemblies, and introducing Code Ninjas to the school. And soon enough, they held the first meeting. At this point, they both had beginner coding knowledge, in HTML, CSS and Python, so while they were introducing coding for the first time to our Code Ninjas, they were also still quite new to theworld of code themselves. Although initially this made the experience quite daunting, they quickly realised that our experience wasn’t as important as the space they were creating for the girls.

Fast forward a few years. The meet-ups, hosted during lunchtimes, are a huge successamong all ages. The girls have a blast learning HTML, CSS, and Python coding, where they have made a website, entered a python coding competition with amazing results from all girls and programmed Lego Mindstorms Robots and Micro:bits.

With other girls graduating from St Ursula’s college in 2019, Tara Forman and myself have become the club leaders for 2019-2021. Not only is Code Ninjas looking forward to another year of fun and coding with a focus on integrating technology into the new St Ursula’s garden, cybersecurity, python coding, tech gadgets including drones, robots, AI technology, and of course more programming competitions, but, the Code Club is now expanding to the Primary school next door - Our Lady of Fatima Catholic Primary school to pass on coding skills to the next generation.
