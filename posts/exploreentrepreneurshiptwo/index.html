Part Two: Here is How to Taste Entrepreneurship

By Dillon de Silva
@dillondesilvaa
September 28 2020 | 2 minute read
Tags: entrepreneurship startups inspiration


Hey everyone!

After reading part one of this article, you are probably wondering how you can get started as an entrepreneur.

Though I am still young and learning, I believe there is four key steps you can take to get started:

Step One: Find something you like

Before diving into trying to capitialise on your skills, figure out what you are good at! Whether it’s technology, art, food, music, socialising or fashion, find something you are passionate about and enjoy. When you are passionate about something, you can generally find associated issues to start your entrepreneurship venture in.

Step Two: Develop a solution

Perhaps you are a really passionate foody and want to share your recipies with the world. Or maybe you feel strongly about raising environmental awareness.

Whatever it is, when you find you are passionate about sometihng, try to think of ways in which you can share that passion with others. Make it unique and fun for yourself.

Find the right tools to develop your solution. Starting a juice business? Make sure you’ve got a blender and are carefully recording the ingredients used.

Step Three: Use Technology (Social Media, Web development, etc)

As Steve Jobs once said, “Marketing is all about values”.

By using social media, creating your own website or having the right contact options for people to reach out to you can make all the difference when trying to start a side hustle as a young person. Some great tools which I would personally recommend include Canva, Wordpress and Instagram

Make sure you connect with your audience and network with others. Who you know and reach out to can make a huge difference when it comes to a startup.

Step Four: Expect Failure

Even if things go wrong, never take it as a negative thing. Being able to try new things, share your creative ideas with the world and try to make a difference is truly an incredible experience. Failure gives us the opportunity to learn and try again. Be proud of yourself for giving something new a go and keep on trying new things. Whilst the journey isn’t always easy, working on your own side hustle can be just about one of the best things you can do for your own self growth. 

    - Dillon
