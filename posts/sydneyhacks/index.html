Sydney Hacks

By Ronaldo keng
@ronaldo_keng
August 12 2020 | 2 minute read
Tags: startup stem inspiration


It was January 2020. I had just returned from NCSS in Melbourne and had won a hackathonat the Academy of Entrepreneurs. The energy of the camps lingered on me and I wasitching to do something, something big, exceptional and meaningful to me. Now, almostevery bit of knowledge I have around programming came from online courses. When thetopics were easy, I cruised through the tutorials, but when my code stopped working, it wastruly frustrating to figure out what’s wrong. I didn’t know anyone taking the same courseand doing the same thing, and after hours of debugging, I often lose motivation and“abandon” the problem.

I felt quite alone in my programming journey.

Does that have to be the case?

I envisioned a community of students, tackling the same problems in the sameenvironment and learning off each other.

This is the origin story of SydneyHacks.

I started SydneyHacks with my mate Emanuel who I’ve worked with sinceTEDxYouth@ReddamHouse 3 years ago. For our first program, we planned to organise abeginner-friendly 24-hour hackathon for high school students all across Sydney, with greatmentors, workshops and an endless supply of coffee!

Things were smooth until the pandemic hit us in Sydney. We were advised to postpone the event and were inactive for weeks, whilst I blandly hoped that the pandemic would pass away.

Which it did not.

It was only then that we chose to pivot into an online challenge! We chose to launch an 8 week online course about Python and luckily, with our incredible team, we pulled it off in less than a month, onboarding 41 students and securing 2 sponsors! The challenge ended not long ago in July and I am grateful for the opportunity to teach others, where everyone is doing the same project.

Our motto for the challenge was “Help is always with you”, as I didn’t want anyone to be stuck on a problem and struggle like I did without a helping hand. SydneyHacks is on a wild adventure and I cannot wait to see what we get up to next. Whilst our team prepares for HSC and yearly exams, we wish you the best luck in your preparations and encourage you to use your expertise to help someone in need. After all,that’s what makes a community strive, and you have everything it takes to initiate that small difference which makes your community a better place. Remember, help is always with you! If you take away anything here, try and be the catalyst of positive change in your community!

    - Ronaldo
